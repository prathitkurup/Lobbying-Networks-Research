INFLATION DIAGNOSIS: Cartesian Product Bug in Edge Construction
======================================================================

-- Step 1: Quantify Raw Duplication --
  Rows per (firm, bill) pair:
    Mean:   3.30
    Median: 3.0
    Max:    20
  Under the bug, for a pair of firms with R_A and R_B rows on a
  shared bill, the loop produces R_A × R_B records instead of 1.
  Expected median inflation factor ≈ 9.0x (median R^2).

-- Step 2: Buggy vs. Fixed Edge Weights --
  Building edges with buggy code (no dedup)...
  Building edges with fixed code (dedup + canonical)...

  BUGGY edges:  43,892 pairs
    weight stats:  mean=12.9  median=6.0  max=1197

  FIXED edges:  24,831 pairs
    weight stats:  mean=2.8  median=2.0  max=125

  Inflation factor at median: 3.0x  (6 buggy / 2 fixed)

-- Step 3: Example Pair --
  Worst inflated pair:
    CARDINAL HEALTH  ↔  STANLEY BLACK & DECKER
    Fixed weight:  1 shared bills
    Buggy weight:  112 (≈112x inflated)

-- Step 4: Canonical Pair Ordering --
  Non-canonical pairs in buggy output: 21,959
  (source > target — these are stored separately from their
   canonical counterpart, causing double-counting.)
  Fixed output: 0 non-canonical pairs (guaranteed by src<tgt constraint).

-- Summary of Fixes --
  1. drop_duplicates(subset=['fortune_name','bill_number'])
     Reduces 25,676 raw rows to 7,392 unique (firm, bill) pairs.
  2. src, tgt = (a,b) if a<b else (b,a)
     Canonical ordering ensures (A,B) and (B,A) always merge.
  Result: median shared-bill count corrected from 6 → 2.

  See design_decisions.md §1-2 for full rationale.
