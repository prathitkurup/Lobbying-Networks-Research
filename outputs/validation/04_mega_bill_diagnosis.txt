  ★ Chosen threshold: MAX_BILL_DF = 50
    Removes 11 bills (0.5% of all bills)
    Accounts for 69.7% of all co-lobbying edges
    Natural break: bills with df > 50 are 50–198 firm omnibus bills;
    bills with df ≤ 45 are industry-specific legislation.

-- Step 4: Modularity Collapse Without Filtering --
  Without filtering (all bills included):
    Network density:    ~68%  (near-complete graph)
    Leiden modularity:  Q ≈ 0.02  (indistinguishable from random)
    Community count:    typically 3–4 non-meaningful communities

  With MAX_BILL_DF = 50 filtering:
    Network density:    ~20%  (sparse, meaningful)
    Leiden modularity:  Q ≈ 0.18  (meaningful community structure)
    Community count:    5–7 industry-aligned clusters

  The Q collapse from 0.18 → 0.02 without filtering is why mega-bill
  removal is necessary for meaningful community detection.

-- Step 5: Two-Stage Filtering for Cosine and RBO Similarity --
  Cosine and RBO use a two-stage filter (unlike the affiliation network):

  Stage 1 (keep all bills): Compute total_budget and fracs.
    frac_ib = firm i's total spend on bill b / firm i's total budget
    This preserves the economic meaning: frac is the share of the
    firm's entire lobbying portfolio allocated to this bill.

  Stage 2 (filter mega-bills): Build cosine/RBO from filtered bills only.
    Rationale: if both firms allocate 0.002 of their budget to CARES Act,
    cosine would treat this as genuine directional alignment despite no
    strategic targeting.  Filtering removes this spurious signal while
    leaving intact the frac denominator (total budget) for all bills.
